Punicaceae


The Punicaceae family represents dicotyledonous plants that include only 2 species and only 1 genus, Punica, which is part of the order Myrtales. The Punicaceae are deciduous fruit trees. The Punica granatum species represents the common pomegranate, while the Punica protopunica species represents the Socotra grenadier, an endemic species of the island of Socotra in the China Sea. The fruits of the latter are smaller and less sweet than those of the common pomegranate. They are also pink, while those of the common pomegranate tree are red. Punicaceae can grow up to 7 meters tall and feature glossy green leaves.
