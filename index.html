About Us.

Extending over an area of​​approximately 17 Ha, containing a floristic richness estimated at more than 600 species, and more than 1000 varieties, the trial garden is undoubtedly the largest public garden in Rabat. It inspired the creation of the botanical gardens of Marrakech (25 Ha and that of Meknes (30 Ha)).
It was created in 1914 by Jean Claude Nicolas Forestier and drawn by a rigorous layout. The plan of the garden was established taking into account the main axes of circulation on the scale of the city, in particular those linking the city center to the districts subsequently developed (Agdal, the orange trees, etc.).
